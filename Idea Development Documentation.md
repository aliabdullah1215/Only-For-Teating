# Idea Development Documentation

## Data Diet Project

---

# 1. Introduction

This document presents the idea development process for the **Data Diet** project. It explains the problem the platform aims to solve, identifies the target users, and outlines the purpose of documenting the idea development and selection of the Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

Many individuals face difficulties accessing reliable and personalized nutrition guidance. Most nutritional information available online is generic and does not consider individual health conditions, lifestyle, or personal health goals. In addition, professional nutrition consultations are often expensive, difficult to access, or inconvenient for many users. As a result, people frequently follow unsuitable diet plans or unhealthy routines without proper guidance.

The **Data Diet** project addresses these challenges by introducing an AI-powered digital nutrition platform that provides personalized dietary recommendations based on user data and health goals. The platform also connects users directly with certified nutrition specialists and fitness coaches, creating an integrated environment where users can receive professional advice, track their progress, and improve their overall health and lifestyle.

The main target users of the platform include:

- Individuals seeking personalized nutrition guidance  
- Certified nutrition specialists who provide dietary consultations  
- Professional fitness coaches who support users with workout and lifestyle recommendations  

The purpose of this documentation is to describe the idea development process behind the **Data Diet** platform, explain the problem it addresses, identify its target users, and outline how the system leverages artificial intelligence and professional expertise to deliver personalized nutrition and fitness solutions that promote healthier lifestyles.

---

# 2. Brainstorming Process

The idea development process for the **Data Diet** project began with a series of collaborative brainstorming sessions involving all team members. These sessions combined open discussions with **mind mapping techniques** to generate, organize, and refine potential project ideas. This approach encouraged active participation from every team member and allowed ideas to be visually structured and expanded in a clear and systematic way.

To enhance creativity and ensure a structured ideation process, the team applied several brainstorming and analytical techniques. These methods helped the team analyze problems from different perspectives, explore possible solutions, and evaluate the feasibility and potential impact of each idea.

The techniques used during the brainstorming process included:

- Brainwriting  
- 5 Whys Analysis  
- Starbursting  
- Mind Mapping  
- Rolestorming / Figure Storming  
- Gap Filling (Gap Analysis)  
- Brain-netting  
- Round Robin Brainstorming  
- Rapid Ideation  
- Reverse Brainstorming  
- Stepladder Brainstorming  
- SWOT Analysis  

During these brainstorming sessions, the team generated and evaluated **seven different project ideas**, each analyzed based on innovation, feasibility, technical complexity, and potential user impact.

After careful evaluation, the team selected the **Data Diet – AI-Powered Digital Nutrition Specialist Platform** as the most promising concept because it provides an innovative solution to a real and widespread problem related to healthy lifestyle management.

The final decision was reached through **comparative analysis, elimination of less feasible ideas, and collective discussion**, ultimately leading the team to agree on the Data Diet concept as the most impactful and feasible solution.

# 3. Ideas Considered

## Idea 1 — Automotive Directory Platform

### Description
A digital platform designed to provide users with comprehensive information about different car models, including specifications, prices, reviews, and related services such as dealerships and maintenance centers. The platform would help users compare vehicles and make informed purchasing decisions.

### Strengths

- Provides organized and accessible information about car models  
- Helps users compare vehicles before purchasing  
- Useful for car buyers and enthusiasts  

### Weaknesses

- Many similar platforms already exist in the market  
- Requires continuous updates to maintain accurate car data  
- Limited innovation compared to other ideas  

### Reason for Rejection

The idea was rejected because it lacks strong innovation and similar solutions are already widely available, making it difficult to differentiate the project.

## Idea 2 — Investment Banking Wallet Application

### Description
A financial application designed to help users manage their investments, monitor financial portfolios, and perform digital banking operations through a secure mobile platform.

### Strengths

- Addresses the growing demand for digital financial services  
- Could help users manage investments conveniently  
- Potential for integration with financial analytics tools  

### Weaknesses

- Requires strict compliance with financial regulations  
- High security requirements and complex infrastructure  
- Integration with banking systems can be difficult  

### Reason for Rejection

The idea was rejected because it involves complex banking regulations, security requirements, and integrations with financial institutions, making it difficult to implement within the project scope.

# 4. Selected MVP Idea

## Data Diet – AI Nutrition Assistant

The **Data Diet – AI Nutrition Assistant** was selected as the project's **Minimum Viable Product (MVP)**. The system focuses on delivering the core functionality of the platform while maintaining a manageable development scope.

### Key Features

- Personalized nutrition recommendations using AI  
- User health profile management  
- Basic progress tracking for health goals  
- Consultation requests with nutrition specialists or fitness coaches  
- AI-based daily meal suggestions  

### Rationale for Selecting This MVP

The MVP focuses on the essential value of the platform: **personalized nutrition guidance powered by artificial intelligence**. By implementing the most critical features first, the team can validate the idea quickly and collect user feedback while keeping development complexity manageable.

### Expected Impact

The MVP will provide users with accessible and personalized nutrition guidance, helping them make healthier dietary decisions. It also demonstrates the feasibility of integrating **AI-driven health insights with professional consultation services** within a single platform.
